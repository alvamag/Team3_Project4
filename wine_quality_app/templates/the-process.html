The Process

Here, we outline how our machine learning model was trained and tested to predict wine quality, including the dataset and methodologies used.

Data Cleaning & Feature Engineering

We removed null values, merged the datasets, applied feature scaling, and saved the result as cleaned_wine_data.csv.

Spark SQL Analysis

White wines scored slightly higher on average; alcohol is a strong indicator of quality, while density shows a negative correlation with quality.

Model Preparation and Binning

Class encoding was defined as follows: 0 = low quality (scores 3–5), 1 = medium quality (score 6), 2 = high quality (scores 7+).

Initial Model Training

We used Random Forest as our baseline model, achieving 88.62% accuracy. Performance was evaluated using precision, recall, and F1 score.

Model Optimization

We experimented with Logistic Regression, SVM, and PCA. Random Forest consistently performed best with minimal dimensionality reduction.

Final Model Evaluation

The final model maintained strong performance across all metrics and generalized well, with a final accuracy of 88.62% on test data.
