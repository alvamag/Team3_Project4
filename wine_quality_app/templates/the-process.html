The Process

Here, we outline how our machine learning model was trained and tested to predict wine quality, including the dataset and methodologies used.

Data Cleaning & Feature Engineering

We removed null values, merged the datasets, applied feature scaling, and saved the result as cleaned_wine_data.csv.

Spark SQL Analysis

White wines scored slightly higher on average; alcohol is a strong indicator of quality, while density shows a negative correlation with quality.

Model Preparation and Model Binning

Class encoding was defined as follows: 0 represents low quality (scores 3–5), 1 represents medium quality (score 6), and 2 represents high quality (scores 7 and above).

Initial Model Training

We used Random Forest as our baseline model, achieving an accuracy of 88.62%, and evaluated its performance using precision, recall, and F1 score.

Initial Model Optimization

We experimented with Logistic Regression and SVM, applied PCA to reduce dimensionality, and found that Random Forest performed best overall.

Initial Model Evaluation

The model achieved high precision and recall for most classes, generalized well to unseen data, and reached a final accuracy of 88.62%.
